Create a short animated video (11–18 seconds), social media style, vertical format (9:16).

IDEA (hook using psychological triggers):
Use fear + simplicity + relatability:
People are silently losing their vision every day while working at a computer, but a very simple habit can prevent it.

STYLE:
Minimalistic cartoon character, clean design, soft colors, smooth transitions.
Character is the same in all scenes, only poses change.

SCENE 1 (Hook – fear + contradiction):
A cartoon character sits at a computer, staring intensely at the screen.
Text on screen:
"You are losing your eyesight right now..."
Pause for impact (1–2 seconds).
Add subtle eye strain visual effect (blur or glow).

SCENE 2 (Relatability):
Zoom slightly closer.
Character rubs eyes, looks tired.
Text on screen:
"8 hours at the screen = tired eyes"

SCENE 3 (Solution – simplicity):
Character changes pose, sits more relaxed.
Soft lighting improves.
Text on screen:
"One simple habit can fix this"

SCENE 4 (Action – demonstration):
Character looks away from the screen toward a window (or distance).
Text on screen:
"Look away every 20 minutes"

SCENE 5 (Result – positive future):
Character now looks happy, relaxed, confident.
Eyes look clear and healthy.
Text on screen:
"Clear vision. Less fatigue."

SCENE 6 (Final CTA):
Minimal background.
Text centered on screen:
"Take care of your eyes today"

OPTIONAL small subtitle:
"Based on real eye care recommendations"

TRANSITIONS:
Use soft fade-to-black between scenes.

TIMING:
Fast pacing, each scene ~2–3 seconds.

MOOD:
Helpful, slightly alarming at start, then reassuring and positive.

QUALITY:
smooth animation, stable frames, clean typography, no distortion, high clarity
